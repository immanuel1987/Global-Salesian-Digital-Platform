How the portal works
Two ways in: precise search (like a library) and gentle exploration (collections + optional assistant) — serious, human, never “commercial AI”.

📚 Structured search

Use filters and the search bar to query the catalogue the way archivists organise it: theme, document type, language, macro-region, year, author, and publisher.

  • Each record shows type, author, year, category, and thematic tags.
  • Open access by default: thumbnails, full PDFs, and (when available) chapters or sections.
  • Complex queries can be saved and shared via the URL — bookmark or send a link.

✦ Smart search & assistant

“Smart search” means the system can follow meaning (topics, related phrases, context) — not only exact keywords. It complements the catalogue; it does not replace it.

The assistant stays in the corner: ask questions, get suggestions, then open real documents. Sign in later if you need advanced features.

  • Try: “Preventive System in Latin America (1950–1970)” or browse Collections when you don’t have exact words.

About Us

GSDP is the official digital knowledge platform of the Salesians of Don Bosco — built to make the vast Salesian intellectual heritage freely explorable by scholars, educators, ministers, and the curious, worldwide.

In South Asia alone, Don Bosco runs 261 schools, 138 technical institutes, 174 Young-at-Risk centres, and 192 hostels — serving millions of the poorest young people across India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Nepal, and beyond.

Smart search follows meaning as well as keywords; the assistant stays optional and visible in the corner — institutional, human, never a “commercial chatbot”.

Project transparency: GSDP is developed as an open-knowledge layer (ontology-backed catalogue, curated collections, role-based access). Roadmap, tech stack, and phases are documented for partners — request the tech note →

Platform overview

Visual tour
Part 1 · What the platform is

Global Salesian Digital Platform (GSDP)

  • One place for Salesian knowledge: documents, good practice, formation material, and media — organised with a shared ontology.
  • Linked to real life: pastoral works (institutions), provinces, events, and people, so context stays visible.
  • Built for the whole Salesian Family — public discovery first; deeper tools after sign-in.
Part 2 · How to use it

Find your way in a few moves

  • Use the top menu (Pastoral Works, Resources, Events, Collections…) or scroll the homepage sections in order.
  • Search with ordinary words — filters narrow by area, province, language, and document type.
  • Sign in with Enter Platform for the full library, exploration assistant, and role-based dashboards.
Part 3 · Lowering the entry barrier

You don’t need a manual to start

  • No account is required to browse public content and understand what is available.
  • This tour + one search or one click on Enter Platform is enough to orient yourself.
  • The assistant (chat bubble) answers in plain language when you are unsure where to look.
Next step

You’re oriented — try it

  • Close this window and use the homepage search, or jump to a section from the menu.
  • Ready for more? Sign in to open saved items, uploads (if your role allows), and analytics.
